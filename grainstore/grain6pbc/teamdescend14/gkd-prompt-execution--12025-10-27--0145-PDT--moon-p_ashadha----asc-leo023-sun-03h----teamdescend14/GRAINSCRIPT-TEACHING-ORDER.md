### **Description**:
I built a knowledge card system where each card is exactly 80 characters wide by 110 lines tall. Perfect for terminals, mobile phones (portrait), tablets (landscape "MOVIE MODE"), and E Ink readers.

We use a permutation-based addressing system (13 consonants, 6-char codes, no duplicates) that gives us exactly 1,235,520 unique cards. Each card teaches ONE concept with patient, hand-holding explanations.

The format works everywhere:
- Your terminal (classic 80-column tradition)
- Your phone (scrollable portrait)
- Your tablet (landscape with black padding like movie shorts)
- Your E Ink reader (high contrast, monospace)

Built with:
- Clojure (grainorder permutation engine)
- Steel (Rust Lisp for scripts)
- Svelte (GitHub Pages site)
- Swiss Ephemeris (astronomical timestamps)

Live demo: https://kae3g.github.io/grainkae3g/grainscript

- If you love monospace aesthetics

- If you want reproducible, portable knowledge cards
