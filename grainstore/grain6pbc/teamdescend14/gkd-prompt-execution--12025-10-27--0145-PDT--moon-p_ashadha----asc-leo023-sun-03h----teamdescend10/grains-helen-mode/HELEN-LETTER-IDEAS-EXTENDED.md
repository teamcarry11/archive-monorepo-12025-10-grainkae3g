## Writing Guidelines for Helen Letters

### Voice & Tone
- First person ("I") addressing Helen directly
- Warm, respectful, student-to-teacher relationship
- Personal anecdotes about learning from her farm
- Questions that invite her wisdom
- Humble acknowledgment of her expertise

### Structure
- Start with farming observation or memory
- Connect to technical concept through analogy
- Develop the parallel with specific examples
- Show how farming wisdom illuminates tech problem
- Close with gratitude or reflection

### Language
- Flowing Oxford prose (not Hemingway terseness)
- Complete elegant sentences
- Only lowercase or UPPERCASE (no camelCase)
- Farming terminology used accurately
- Technical terms explained through farming lens

### Format
- Exactly 110 lines within 80Ã—110 ASCII box
- Links at TOP (file path and live URL)
- Card number and `>` at bottom
- Metadata after closing fence

### Pedagogical Approach
- Make tech accessible to non-technical readers
- Show universal patterns across domains
- Value both ecological and computational thinking
- Bridge ancient farming wisdom with modern technology
- Demonstrate that all good design follows natural principles
