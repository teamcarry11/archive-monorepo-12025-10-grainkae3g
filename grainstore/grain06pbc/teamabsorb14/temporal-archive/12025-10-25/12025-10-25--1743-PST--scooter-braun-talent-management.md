## The Taylor Swift Controversy

### **What Happened** (People Also Ask)

**"What did Scooter Braun do to Taylor?"**

Timeline:
1. **2019**: Scooter Braun's company (Ithaca Holdings) bought Big Machine Label Group
2. **Big Machine owned**: Taylor Swift's first 6 album master recordings
3. **Taylor was NOT offered**: Chance to buy her own masters
4. **Public dispute**: Taylor called it her "worst case scenario"
5. **Result**: Taylor began re-recording albums to own new masters ("Taylor's Version")

**"Is Scooter Braun still making money off of Taylor Swift?"**

Answer: Yes, but diminishing:
- He sold the masters to Shamrock Holdings (2020) for ~$300M
- He still profits from original recordings
- BUT Taylor's re-recordings cannibalize original streams
- Fans prefer "Taylor's Version" (ethical choice)

### **The Ethics**

**Taylor's perspective**:
- Artists should own their work
- She wasn't given fair opportunity to buy
- Bullying (Scooter's clients allegedly bullied her)

**Scooter's perspective**:
- Business transaction (legal purchase)
- Offered to talk (she declined)
- He sold the masters eventually

**The cultural shift**:
- More artists demand ownership
- Re-recording becomes viable strategy
- Fans support artist sovereignty

```

                                                   ‚Üì
                              Manager gets % of revenue, Label gets most
```

**Problems**:
1. **Ownership**: Label owns the work, not the artist
2. **Power imbalance**: Young artists don't understand contracts
3. **Locked in**: Long-term deals (often 7-10 albums)
4. **Extractive**: Label takes majority, artist gets crumbs
5. **Opaque**: Royalty accounting is black box

**Even with "good" managers like Scooter**: System is fundamentally extractive.
