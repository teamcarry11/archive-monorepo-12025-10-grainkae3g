## Notes on the Draft

**What works**:
- Direct ask (apprenticeship + work exchange)
- Clear offer (software skills + willingness to learn/work)
- Specific examples (inventory, field planning - things farmers actually need)
- Humble tone (learning, not teaching)
- Respects her time (short, clear)

**What to adjust**:
- Add your real name
- Maybe mention specific Helen Atthowe writing that influenced you
- Include your location specifics (Caspar cabin details if relevant)
- Adjust timeline (when you'd want to visit - spring? summer?)

**What to remove if too much**:
- The P.S. about Grain Network (might seem like sales pitch)
- Software offer entirely (just focus on apprenticeship)

## About Grain Network (If Asked or for P.S.)

**If she asks "What's Grain Network?"**, here's simple explanation:

---

**Simple version** (ELI5, 10,000 common words):

"It's software that helps people own their data and tools instead of renting them from big companies.

Think of it like: If you own your land, you can farm how you want. If you rent, the landlord decides.

Same with software: If you own it, you control it. If you rent it from Google or Microsoft, they control you.

I'm building tools that regular people (farmers, small businesses, vegan companies) can own and control themselves.

For farming specifically: Simple tools that work offline (no internet needed), track your work (plantings, harvest, customers), and you own the data (not stored on some company's server).

All free and open source. Like sharing seeds - anyone can use them, improve them, share them forward."

---

**Why this explanation works**:
- Uses farming analogy (she understands land ownership)
- Avoids jargon (no "Rust", "Nix", "blockchain", "microkernel")
- Practical (tools that solve real problems)
- Values-aligned (ownership, sharing, community)
- Humble (not "revolutionary" or "changing the world")
